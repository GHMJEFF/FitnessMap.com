Upper Body Workouts

Your upper body is essential in developing functional strength. From daily activities to more advanced movements, building strength in this area helps with pushing, lifting, and supporting your body. The exercises below focus on your chest, shoulders, and arms.

Chest Workouts

Bench Press - A classic chest workout targeting the pectoral muscles, triceps, and shoulders.

Form Tip: Keep your feet flat, core tight, and lower the bar slowly to your chest before driving it upward explosively.

Incline Dumbbell Press - This exercise provides a focused upper chest workout.

Form Tip: Adjust the bench to a 45-degree angle, and maintain control of the dumbbells as you press upwards.

Push-ups - A great bodyweight exercise to improve overall upper body strength.

Form Tip: Keep your body in a straight line, engaging your core, and lower your chest until it’s just off the ground.

Cable Fly’s - This isolation movement is excellent for targeting the chest's inner and outer fibers, adding definition.

Form Tip: Form Tip: Use a cable machine with a slight bend in your elbows, bringing the handles together in front of your chest in a controlled manner.

Dips - An advanced bodyweight exercise that targets both the chest and triceps, depending on the body angle.

Form Tip: Lean forward to engage the chest more or keep upright for more triceps activation.

Arm Workouts

Barbell Curl - The king of bicep exercises, the barbell curl helps build thickness and strength in your arms.

Form Tip: Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, keeping elbows close to your body as you curl the barbell upward.

Skull Crushers - This is an excellent isolation exercise for the triceps. Despite the intimidating name, it’s a great tool for arm development.

Form Tip: Lower the bar or dumbbells slowly towards your forehead, keeping your upper arms stationary throughout the movement.

Hammer Curls - Hammer curls target the brachialis and forearms, adding both size and strength to your arms.

Form Tip: Keep your wrists neutral and focus on slow, controlled movements to fully engage the biceps.

Tricep Pushdowns - This exercise isolates the triceps and is excellent for adding strength and size to the back of your arms.

Form Tip: Use a rope or straight bar attachment, keeping your elbows pinned to your sides as you push down the handle.

Overhead Tricep Extensions (With Cable) - This overhead movement targets the long head of the triceps for overall arm development.

Form Tip: Keep your elbows close to your head, lowering the weight behind your neck and pressing upwards in a controlled manner.

Lower Body Workouts

Building a solid lower body is key to improving overall strength and power. From everyday movements like walking to advanced sports activities, a strong lower body improves balance and power. The following workouts focus on your legs and core.

Quadriceps and Hamstrings

Squats - Squats are the ultimate full-leg workout, engaging the quads, hamstrings, glutes, and core.

Form Tip: Maintain a neutral spine, push through your heels, and lower your body until your thighs are parallel to the ground.

Deadlifts - One of the best compound exercises, deadlifts work the hamstrings, glutes, and lower back, promoting overall strength and stability.

Form Tip: Keep the bar close to your body, drive through your heels, and lift with your legs, not your back.

Lunges - Lunges are an excellent unilateral exercise, meaning each leg works independently, helping to correct any strength imbalances. These can be done with a barbell, dumbbells, or without any weight at all.

Form Tip: Keep your chest up, core engaged, and take a large step forward, lowering your back knee towards the ground.

Leg Extensions - This machine-based exercise targets the quadriceps and helps to isolate them for better muscle definition and strength.

Form Tip: Adjust the machine so that your knees are aligned with the pivot point, and extend your legs fully, keeping the motion slow and controlled.

Leg Curls - Leg curls focus on the hamstrings, providing balance between the front and back of the thighs for overall leg strength. Leg curls can either be done laying down, or seated.

Form Tip: Adjust the machine pad so that it rests just above your ankles, and curl your legs slowly, focusing on squeezing your hamstrings.

Glutes

Hip Thrusts - Hip thrusts are fantastic for isolating the glutes and building posterior chain strength. They can be done on a machine but your gym may not have that. Instead, you can also do it with a barbell and a bench.

Form Tip: Keep your back against a bench and drive your hips upwards, squeezing your glutes at the top.

Bulgarian Split Squats - Another fantastic unilateral exercise, the Bulgarian split squat heavily targets the glutes, quads, and hamstrings.

Form Tip: Elevate your rear foot on a bench, keep your core tight, and lower your hips until your front thigh is parallel to the ground.

Training Tips and Recovery

Progressive Overload: One of the key principles of strength training is gradually increasing the resistance over time. This could mean adding more weight, performing more reps, or increasing the number of sets.

Rest and Recovery: Muscles grow when you rest, not while you train. Be sure to incorporate 48 hours of rest for each muscle group worked and prioritize sleep.

Nutrition: Fuel your workouts with the right nutrition. Aim to consume plenty of protein, healthy fats, and complex carbohydrates to help repair and rebuild muscle fibers.
